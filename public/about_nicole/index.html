about nicole

Not all paths are straight paths.

My path to becoming a therapist began in 2006 when I completed my formal education and entered the field of public social work. Over the years, I developed a deep passion for supporting and empowering others. As I engaged in this work, I grew increasingly curious about the whole person—how our emotional, physical, and cognitive responses shape our experiences. ​

Drawn by Curiosity

This curiosity led me to explore whole-body approaches to healing. In 2018, I completed various yoga teacher trainings, including the Trauma Center Trauma-Sensitive Yoga Program (TCTSY) with the Center for Trauma and Embodiment. This training bridged my professional experience with my passion for mind-body healing and opened the door to my work as a therapist.

My Qualifications:

  • Trauma Center Trauma-Sensitive Yoga (TCTSY) - 300-hour program
  • Integrative Somatic Trauma Therapy - 60-hour certificate
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • ADHD-focused trainings
  • Somatic EMDR - 60 hour training
  • Yoga Teacher Training - (500hr)

Outside of Therapy

When I’m not in session, I enjoy spending time with my family and our dog. I also enjoy cooking, reading, and spending time outdoors. You’ll often find me tending to (and occasionally fretting over) my vegetable garden. I also love engaging in physical activities like yoga, walking, and sports.

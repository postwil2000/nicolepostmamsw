somatic therapy

What is Somatic Therapy?

Somatic Therapy is a body-centred approach to psychotherapy that addresses how trauma, stress and emotions are stored in the body. By combining traditional talk therapy with physical awareness and movement, it helps to address the mind-body split and regulate the nervous system, effectively treating PTSD, anxiety and chronic stress.

Somatic therapy serves as a toolkit for helping individuals manage their emotions and transition from a state of fight, flight or freeze to a more controlled state. It focuses on how our thoughts and feelings affect our bodies and employs specific techniques to release tension, anger and frustration stemming from past hurts. 

Who’s it for?

Somatic therapy can be beneficial for a wide range of individuals, particularly those who have experienced trauma, stress, or emotional difficulties. Here are some groups of people who might find somatic therapy helpful:

Trauma Survivors
Individuals who have experienced physical, emotional, or sexual trauma may benefit from somatic therapy, as it helps process and release trauma stored in the body.

People with Anxiety and Depression
Somatic therapy can help individuals manage symptoms of anxiety and depression by promoting relaxation and increasing awareness of bodily sensations.

Chronic Pain Sufferers
By focusing on the connection between mind and body, somatic therapy can help individuals manage chronic pain and improve their overall quality of life.

Those with Stress-Related Conditions
People experiencing high levels of stress may benefit from somatic techniques that help reduce tension and promote a sense of calm.

Individuals with Body Image Issues
Somatic therapy can foster a healthier relationship with the body by increasing awareness and acceptance of bodily sensations.

People with Dissociative Disorders
Somatic approaches can help individuals reconnect with their bodies and reduce dissociative symptoms.

Those Seeking Personal Growth
Individuals interested in deepening their self-awareness and enhancing their emotional well-being may find somatic therapy to be a valuable tool.

Cancer Survivors and Patients
Somatic therapy can help manage the emotional and physical side effects of cancer treatment.

Overall, somatic therapy is suitable for anyone interested in exploring the connection between their physical and emotional experiences and seeking a holistic approach to healing and personal growth.

Core Concepts and Techniques

Somatic Experiencing (SE)
Developed by Dr. Peter Levine, this technique involves tracking physical sensations (such as a racing heart or shallow breathing) while discussing past stressors to help the nervous system process and release trauma.

Body Awareness & Scanning
Mentally scanning the body to identify tension points, numbness or pain, enabling you to recognize your body’s physical cues of stress.

Titration & Pendulation
Gently guiding you through a difficult memory in small doses (titration) while moving your focus back and forth between a stressed and relaxed state (pendulation).

Resourcing
Identifying specific memories, people or places that make you feel safe, utilising them to anchor yourself when processing difficult emotions. 

These techniques, which highlight how we sense our bodies internally and externally, may help alleviate the effects of trauma, anxiety and pain. 

What to expect from a Somatic Therapy Session

Assessment and Goal Setting
At the beginning, your therapist will likely conduct an assessment to understand your history, current issues, and what you hope to achieve through therapy. Together, you'll set goals for your sessions.

Mind-Body Awareness
A key component of somatic therapy is increasing your awareness of bodily sensations. Your therapist may guide you through exercises to help you tune into your body, such as scanning your body for tension or noticing areas of comfort.

Breathing Techniques
Breathing exercises are often used to help regulate the nervous system and increase body awareness. Your therapist may teach you specific breathing techniques to help you manage stress or anxiety.

Movement and Bodywork
Somatic therapy may involve gentle movement, stretching, or bodywork to release physical tension and promote relaxation. Your therapist will work with you to find movements that feel comfortable and beneficial.

Emotional Expression
As you become more aware of your body, you may begin to notice emotions that are connected to physical sensations. Your therapist will support you in expressing these emotions in a safe and supportive environment.

Trauma Processing
If you're working through trauma, somatic therapy can be particularly effective. Your therapist will help you process traumatic experiences in a way that integrates both mind and body, often using techniques like EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) or Somatic Experiencing.

Mindfulness and Relaxation
Mindfulness practices are often incorporated to help you stay present and grounded during sessions. Your therapist may guide you through relaxation exercises to reduce stress and promote a sense of well-being.

A Tailored Approach
Somatic therapy is highly individualized, so your therapist will tailor the sessions to meet your specific needs and preferences. They will adjust techniques and approaches based on your feedback and progress.

Overall, somatic therapy aims to help you develop a deeper understanding of the connection between your thoughts, emotions, and physical sensations, leading to greater self-awareness and healing. It's important to find a therapist who you feel comfortable with and who has experience in somatic therapy.

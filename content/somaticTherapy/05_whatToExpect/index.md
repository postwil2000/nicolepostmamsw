**Assessment and Goal Setting**<br>
At the beginning, your therapist will likely conduct an assessment to understand your history, current issues, and what you hope to achieve through therapy. Together, you'll set goals for your sessions.


**Mind-Body Awareness**<br>
A key component of somatic therapy is increasing your awareness of bodily sensations. Your therapist may guide you through exercises to help you tune into your body, such as scanning your body for tension or noticing areas of comfort.


**Breathing Techniques**<br>
Breathing exercises are often used to help regulate the nervous system and increase body awareness. Your therapist may teach you specific breathing techniques to help you manage stress or anxiety.


**Movement and Bodywork**<br>
Somatic therapy may involve gentle movement, stretching, or bodywork to release physical tension and promote relaxation. Your therapist will work with you to find movements that feel comfortable and beneficial.


**Emotional Expression**<br>
As you become more aware of your body, you may begin to notice emotions that are connected to physical sensations. Your therapist will support you in expressing these emotions in a safe and supportive environment.


**Trauma Processing**<br>
If you're working through trauma, somatic therapy can be particularly effective. Your therapist will help you process traumatic experiences in a way that integrates both mind and body, often using techniques like EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) or Somatic Experiencing.


**Mindfulness and Relaxation**<br>
Mindfulness practices are often incorporated to help you stay present and grounded during sessions. Your therapist may guide you through relaxation exercises to reduce stress and promote a sense of well-being.


**A Tailored Approach**<br>
Somatic therapy is highly individualized, so your therapist will tailor the sessions to meet your specific needs and preferences. They will adjust techniques and approaches based on your feedback and progress.


Overall, somatic therapy aims to help you develop a deeper understanding of the connection between your thoughts, emotions, and physical sensations, leading to greater self-awareness and healing. It's important to find a therapist who you feel comfortable with and who has experience in somatic therapy.
